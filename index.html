HackOHI/O 2019

For me, exploring the computer science industry meant finally joining my friends in their 24-hour hackathons at Ohio State. This past fall, I joined a team with a few friends at HackOHI/O, a 24-hour programming marathon for Ohio State students. Over 800 people joined the competition and worked in the Ohio Union all weekend on various programming projects and company-sponsored challenges. My team and I developed an iOS app to recommend clothing choices to users based on weather and style. The app used Microsoft Azure Computer Vision technology to categorize users’ clothing from an uploaded image. It was a great learning experience for me, learning how to use tools like Microsoft Azure and building software in a team. In the end, we won “Best New to Hack” award for developing an impressive product with no previous hackathon experience.

Brain Health Hack 2020

My second hackathon experience slightly overlapped with my interests in the biotech field. At the 2020 Brain Health Hack at OSU’s Wexner Medical Center, my friends and I worked in a team to develop an app that would help PTSD victims log and track their symptoms. The app, SymptomWatch, allowed users to log their symptoms after an episode or attack, keep track of how often they occurred, export the information to be sent to their doctor, and call an emergency contact if needed. The purpose of the app was to help victims of PTSD better communicate with their doctor and monitor their progress, allowing adjustments to treatment if needed. In the end, we got to present to a handful of healthcare professionals at The Wex, and the project won an award for “Most Technically Advanced.”
